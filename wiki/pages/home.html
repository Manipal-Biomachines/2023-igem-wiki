CARBANEL

Pharmaceutical and personal care products (PPCPs) are consumed globally at a rate of 0.1 million tonnes per year. Triclocarban (TCC) is a popularly incorporated antimicrobial within PPCPs since it prevents product spoilage and infection to the consumer.







Once flushed off into the environment due to inefficient degradation in wastewater treatment plants, TCC poses endocrine-disruptive threats to hinder proper metabolism amongst fish & crops.







Moreover, currently implemented degradation methods degrade TCC into 4-chloroaniline and 3,4-dichloroaniline which are also toxic and associated with mutagenesis.







Our solution:

CarbanEl uses multiple enzymes in tandem. TCC-2 from Ochrobactrum sp. degrades TCC to 4-chloroaniline and 3,4-dichloroaniline. The associated gene, tccA, is introduced into a chassis which naturally degrades the chloroaniline byproducts into non-toxic intermediates of the Krebs cycle.
